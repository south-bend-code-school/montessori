At the Poles

There is no question that the many animals living in the poles are hardy, considering the extreme conditions to which they have adapted. However, they have adapted to a very particular range of climates, and this delicate balance has been upset by the emission of greenhouse gasses. In fact, by the year 2040, the Arctic sea ice may dissapear entirely during the summer months. Many animals, including Polar Bears and Pacific Walruses, rest on the ice either between hunts or while hunting. Now, with the melting of the ice, they have no place to rest. In addition to heating the world, greenhouse gasses also increase the acidity of the oceans, causing the shells of various planktons (and some corals) to dissolve, robbing some whales, herring, and cod of their main food source. In addition, various animals such as caribou are changing their migration patterns, causing great difficulties for communities near the poles. Finally, the melting of the polar ice comprises a vicious cycle. Since polar ice reflects more waterthan does dark seawater, this melting only warms the Earth further.
