At the Glaciers

Glaciers worldwide are undergoing a drastic metdown due to Climate change. In general, glaciers gain mass through deposition of snow and lose mass through melting and sublimation. In general, these factors cancel each other, but recently, due to global warming, this balance has been upset. Some statistics are as follows:




The total loss of glacier mass from 1980 to 2016 is -19.9 meters.


In 2017 alone, the average mass loss for all glaciers surveyed was 41 inches, and only three of them increased in mass.
